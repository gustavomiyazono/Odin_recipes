Shabbat-don ( シャバト ドン )



What is Shabbat-don?


Shabbat don is our family's version of the classic Tamago-don donburi (Japanese rice bowl) that we typically make during friday to be enjoyed during Shabbat (friday after sunset until saturday before sunset). Our iteration of the traditional Japanese Tamago-don features a paté of boiled eggs, grated garlic, green onion and gari, over shiro-gohan (Japanese rice).


This recipe makes two servings.

Ingredients

  • 5 eggs
  • 1 clove of garlic
  • 1/3 cup of chopped green onions
  • 2 cups of cooked japanese white rice
  • Gari (japanese pickled ginger)
  • Soy sauce (traditional japanese type)
  • Salt
  • Optional: Crushed povilho biscuits (brazilian sour cassava starch biscuits)
  • Optional: Hot sauce

Steps

  1. Boil water in a pan, then place the eggs carefully in the boiling water and leave them for 8 minutes. (pro tip: add a dash of white vinegar to the water to avoid a mess in case one of the eggs are slightly cracked) Remove the eggs and place them in a bowl of icy water to stop the cooking process.
  2. Peel the boiled eggs and add them to a bowl, with a fork smash the eggs into a homogenic paté.
  3. To the egg salad bowl add a grated clove of garlic, chopped green onions and salt to taste.
  4. In the serving bowl add the shiro-gohan (cooked japanese rice), top it with the egg salad, add a dash of shoyu (soy sauce) and some slices of gari (pickled ginger).
  5. Optional step: If desired, for added taste and texture you can add crushed polvilho biscuits and hot sauce to taste.
